Olympics

Olympics are the international level sports event where players from all over the world meet at a place and compete in various sports. All the players give their best and even participating and getting qualified for Olympics requires a lot of investment of sweat and blood.

Olympics Symbols:

Olympics Motto:

As the official motto of the Olympic Games, Coubertin adopted “Citius, altius, fortius,” Latin for “Faster, higher, stronger,”

Olympics Flag:

In the stadium and its immediate surroundings, the Olympic flag is flown freely together with the flags of the participating countries. It has a white background, and in the center, there are five interlaced rings—blue, yellow, black, green, and red. The blue ring is farthest left, nearest the pole. These rings represent the “five parts of the world” joined together in the Olympic movement. Here 5 colors represent 5 continents of the world neglecting Antarctica and considering North and South America as one.
  • Blue ring represents Europe
  • Yellow ring represents Asia
  • Black ring represents Africa
  • Green ring represents Australia
  • Red ring represents America (North and South)

Olympics Mascot:

Each edition of the Olympic Games has had its own distinctive mascot, sometimes more than one. Typically, the mascot is derived from characters or animals especially associated with the host country.

India at Olympics

History


Indian athletes have won 35 medals, all at the Summer Games. For a period of time, India national field hockey team was dominant in Olympic competition, winning eleven medals in twelve Olympics between 1928 and 1980. The run included 8 gold medals in total and six successive gold medals from 1928–1956
The nation sent its largest-ever contingent of 124 competitors to the 2020 Games. To date, the 2020 Summer Olympics are the most successful Games for India since its first regular Olympics appearance in 1920, with Indian Olympians winning 7 medals (1 gold, 2 silver and 4 bronze).

India Participated with 128 athletes in 18 sports fighting in 69 events

In the men's javelin throw, Neeraj Chopra won India's first-ever gold medal in athletics, the nation's second ever individual gold and first individual gold since 2008. P. V. Sindhu won a bronze in women's badminton to became the first Indian female athlete and second Indian to win two consecutive Olympic medals for individual events while the Men's national field hockey team won the bronze medal, their first Olympic medal since 1980.Meerabai Chanu had bagged silver in 49kg weight-lifting and Lovlina borgohain had bagged a bronze medal in Women.Ravi Dhaiya has won a silver medal in 57kg wrestling .The men's 4 x 400m relay team set a new Asian record of 3:00:25. Aditi Ashok in women's golf and Deepak Punia in men's freestyle wrestling (86 kg) both placed fourth in final rankings, as did the women's national field hockey team, which achieved its best performance since its Olympics debut in 1980.

Medal Winners

Indian athletes bagged 7 medals that included 1 Gold,2 Silver and 3 Bronze

Neeraj Chopra bagged gold medal in Men's Javelin throw by throwing a record throw of 87.58m and became 2nd Indian to get individual gold medal in Olympics.

Meerabai Chanu bagged silver medal in Women's 49kg weight-lifting. She won the first medal for India at Tokyo Olympics.

Ravi Dhaiya won Silver in Men's 57kg wrestling. He is the second wrester to bag second position in Olympics after Sushil Kumar

P. V Sindhu won bronze medal in Women's Singles Badminton. She became the only Indian Badminton player to bag two Olympic medals. Before this bronze she had won silver medal in Rio Olympics 2016.

Lovlina Borgohain won a boxing bronze medal on her Olympics debut. She became third Indian Boxer to win an Olympic medal after Vijender Singh and Mary Kom

Bajrang Punia won a bronze medal in 65kg Men's freestyle wrestling. He won the medal while struggling with knee injury happened just before the event started.

Indian Men's Hockey team ended the dry run of 41 years by winning a bronze medal in Men's Field hockey in Olympics after the Moscow Olympics 1980.
